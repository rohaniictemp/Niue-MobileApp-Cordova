Alofi Tokelau (Alofi North)

Alofi is the capital of the Pacific Ocean island nation of Niue. With a population of 597 in 2017, Alofi has the distinction of being the second smallest national capital city in terms of population (after Ngerulmud, capital of Palau). It consists of the two villages: Alofi North and Alofi South where the government headquarters are located.

Makefu

Makefu is one of the fourteen villages of Niue. Its population at the 2017 census was 70, up from 64 in 2011

Tuapa

Tuapa is one of the fourteen villages of Niue. Its population at the 2017 census was 112, up from 97 in 2011

Namukulu

Namukulu is one of the fourteen villages of Niue. With a population of 11 (2017 census), it is the smallest village on the island.

Hikutavake

Hikutavake is one of the fourteen villages of Niue. Its population at the 2017 census was 49, up from 40 in 2011.

Toi

Toi is one of the fourteen villages of Niue, located north of the island, 17 km from Alofi. Its population at the 2017 census was 22, down from 25 in 2011.

Mutalau

Mutalau is one of the fourteen villages of Niue. Its population at the 2017 census was 100, up from 97 in 2011

Lakepa

Lakepa is one of the fourteen villages of Niue. Its population at the 2017 census was 87, up from 70 in 2011.

Liku

Liku is one of the fourteen villages of Niue, located close to the easternmost part of the island. It lies due east of the capital, Alofi, and its population at the 2017 census was 98.

Hakupu

Hakupu is one of the fourteen villages of the island of Niue. According to the 2017 census, it has a population of 220, making it the second-largest village in Niue

Vaiea

Vaiea is one of the fourteen villages of Niue. Its population was 115 as of 2017.

Avatele

Avatele, formerly known as Oneonepata Matavaihala, is one of the fourteen villages of Niue, located on the southwest coast, with a population of 143 residents as of 2017

Tamakautoga

Tamakautoga is one of the fourteen villages within the Pacific Ocean island nation of Niue. Tamakautoga is located in the southwestern portion of the island and borders the villages of Avatele, Hakupu, and Niue's capital, Alofi, meeting all three at a quadripoint. The village's population at the 2017 census was 160, up from 136 in 2011. This number has fallen significantly from the 19th century which was reported to be 275 in an 1899 mission census. Tamakautoga is represented by Andrew Funaki in the Niue Assembly.
